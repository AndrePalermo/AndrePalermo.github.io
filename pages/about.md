I am a researcher in high-energy nuclear theory, currently working at the [Institut Denis Poisson](https://www.idpoisson.fr/) in Tours.   
My research focuses on spin physics in the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions, using methods of quantum field theory, relativistic statistical mechanics, and relativistic hydrodynamics. <br/>

I completed my PhD on *Spin polarization in the strongly interacting QCD matter at global and local equilibrium* with a cotutelle (joint PhD) between the Florence University and the Frankfurt Goethe University, and I joined the Stony Brook University for my first postdoc.

<a href="https://flore.unifi.it/retrieve/33073af1-4d90-41fc-a903-39ee4cf54c5a/tesi.pdf"><i class="fa-regular fa-newspaper"></i> You can check out my thesis here.</a>

I have been awarded a Marie Skłodowska-Curie Fellowship with my project SPINNERET, supported by the European Union.

## SPINNERET - Spin Polarization in NoN-Equilibreted RelativisTic plasmas

This project has three main objectives:

* Understand and resolve the pseudogauge ambiguity in non-equilibrium relativistic statistical mechanics.
* Calculate the exact polarization of a boost invariant fluid of Dirac fermions.
* Understand how the Eigenstate Thermalization Hypothesis can be applied to relativistic equilibrium with acceleration.
